The Overberg Toyota Owners club, though new has established a widely known and respectable reputation across the Western Cape car club community. The group has also participated in many car club competition and has also hosted one of their own. The group has also hosted several fund raising events for which the proceeds have gone towards soup kitchens and charities based in the Overberg district.


histrory

The Overberg Toyota Owners club is as new car club established on the 15 February 2019. The club started with a group of 5 friend who were tired of the of the sloppy car clubs they were in. These car clubs were unorganised and the members did not participate in any of the clubs events, thus the clubs became uneventful and ultimately ended. The friends came together and decided to start their own car club where they would personally organise the club and members would have to participate. The club president Dylan Arendse and his friends quickly expanded the group to over 50 members by May, but faced some problems when some member started to argue amongst each other. The club president decided to kick out the troublesome members and recruit others. Through the later months the club grew and gained a renowned reputation of being well organised and well supported by the Overberg community. The club has still been thriving through the COVID-19 lockdown and has become a pillar in the Overberg community.


mission

The Overberg Toyota Owners clubs mission is to uplift the community while displaying and feeding their love for their cars. The club organises many car based events were money is raised for the club to help the under privileged and donate to the charities located in the Overberg district.


vision

The clubs main focus is to give back to the community. The club has been donating to many charities and has helped many under privileged people, thus the clubs goal is to increase their role in the community. The clubs vision is to be the biggest car club in the Overberg region and to have the most effect on the Overberg communities.
